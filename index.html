Simulating multicellular life in 3D

I developed a Python library, named Goo, to simulate cell mechanics, signal transduction and gene regulatory networks in 3D inside Blender. I led a team of biologists, doctors and computational biologists to build this unique one-stop artifical life environment.

About me

Antoine A. Ruzette

I strive for research so beautiful it is mistaken for arts, and arts so technical it is mistaken for science.

My formal education is Bioengineering, then Bioinformatics and Computer Science. My research has spanned over the years: machine learning for bacterial genome mining, bioimage analysis and computer vision for microscopy image analysis, simulation of embryonic development in 3D, and medical device development for plastic surgery and biosensors.
